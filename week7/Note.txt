Semantic tag:

nav
aside
header
article
footer

<header>: Represents the introductory content of a section or a page, typically containing headings, logos, and navigation menus.

<nav>: Marks a section of a webpage that contains navigation links, such as menus or lists of links to other pages.

<main>: Defines the main content of a webpage. There should be only one <main> element per page.

<article>: Represents a self-contained composition within a document, such as a blog post, news article, or forum post.

<section>: Groups related content together and typically contains a heading. It's used to structure the content of a page.

<aside>: Defines content that is tangentially related to the content around it, like a sidebar or additional information.

<footer>: Represents the footer section of a page or a section, typically containing copyright information, contact details, and other metadata.

<figure>: Contains elements like images, illustrations, diagrams, or videos, often accompanied by a <figcaption> for a caption.

<figcaption>: Used in conjunction with a <figure> element to provide a caption or description for the content it contains.

<time>: Represents a specific point in time or a range of time and can be used to mark up dates and times on a page.

<mark>: Indicates text that has been highlighted or marked for reference within the document.
